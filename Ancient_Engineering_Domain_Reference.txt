SECTION 1 — METHODOLOGY: SYSTEMS ARCHAEOLOGY
============================================================

1.1 PASSIVE PRECISION HYPOTHESIS (PPH)
Ancient builders achieved extraordinary precision not through extraordinary skill alone,
but through systems designed so that precision emerged inevitably from physical constraints.
Gravity, fluid mechanics, Euclidean geometry, and material properties did the hard work.
The system was the primary precision instrument — not the individual.

Three sub-claims:
(a) The precision of structures such as Giza is empirically verified and real.
(b) The mechanisms that produced it were physical, not mystical.
(c) Those mechanisms were deliberately designed into the construction system.

1.2 THE SIX MECHANISMS (UNIVERSAL PRECISION TOOLKIT)
M1 — Hydraulic Leveling: Still water surface is perpendicular to gravity. Always.
     Equation: P = rho * g * h (fluid statics)
     Application: Basin leveling, channel datum, settlement detection.

M2 — Gravity-Plumb Constraint: A suspended mass hangs vertical. Always.
     Application: Vertical reference, plumb bobs, drift detection.

M3 — Stellar and Solar Alignment: Fixed astronomical references for cardinal orientation.
     Application: True north establishment, face alignment to within arcminutes.

M4 — Geometric Self-Correction: Constraint propagation bounds error accumulation.
     The system detects and corrects errors before they lock in.
     Key condition: correction_capacity / expected_degradation >= 1 + margin

M5 — Material Compliance: Gypsum mortar plastic during setting, rigid after.
     Allows fine-tuning at placement, then locks.
     Material: gypsum harder than copper, softer than limestone.

M6 — Perimeter Bootstrapping: Each course inherits its reference from the course below.
     Error propagation is bounded rather than free-running.

Universal adoption: M2 and M4 appear in every stacked-element construction system, everywhere.
     They are not choices — they are physics.

1.3 SYSTEMS ARCHAEOLOGY PROTOCOL
Step 1: Quantify the anomaly precisely (not "very precise" — state the actual deviation)
Step 2: Propose a physical mechanism capable of producing that precision
Step 3: Verify simultaneous constraint satisfaction (one mechanism, all parameters)
Step 4: State the falsification condition (what observation would disprove it)
Step 5: Cross-cultural validation (independent discovery = physical necessity)
Step 6: Decline diagnostics (mechanism abandonment = specific precision degradation)

1.4 ONTOLOGICAL HIERARCHY
Level 1 — Artifact: physical object, material properties
Level 2 — Structure: geometric relationships, positional information
Level 3 — System: operating mechanisms, construction constraints, feedback loops
Level 4 — Meta-System: governing physical laws (fluid statics, gravity, Euclidean geometry, material mechanics)
Systems Archaeology operates at Levels 3 and 4.

1.5 CONSTRAINT GATE FORMAT
Every claim must pass through:
  gate -> budget -> ratio -> conflict check -> falsifier -> validation protocol

  Gate: the specific physical function that must work
  Budget: available force, time, space, energy, precision, or material
  Ratio: available / required (ratio < 1.0 FAILS)
  Binding constraint: min(ratio_i) across all gates
  Falsifier: the specific measurement that would collapse the gate

1.6 CLAIM BOUNDARY DISCIPLINE
Systems Archaeology CAN establish:
  - Physical mechanisms capable of producing observed precision
  - That a mechanism satisfies all constraints simultaneously
  - Specific observations that would falsify a hypothesis
  - That no alternative mechanism with equivalent explanatory power exists
  - The pattern of precision decline mapped to mechanism abandonment

Systems Archaeology CANNOT establish:
  - That any mechanism was actually used historically
  - Why builders chose their methods (intent, cosmology, religion)
  - That convergence proves contact between civilizations
  - Historical construction sequence without direct archaeological evidence

SECTION 2 — MATH SPINE: UNIVERSAL EQUATIONS
============================================================

All equations are dimensional. Always check units. Never skip unit verification.

2.1 STATICS AND MASS
  Weight:               W = m * g           (N; m in kg, g = 9.81 m/s²)
  Bearing stress:       sigma = W / A       (Pa = N/m²; convert: 1 MPa = 1,000,000 Pa)
  Factor of safety:     FoS = capacity / demand
  Overturning moment:   M_overturn = F_h * h  (N·m)
  Restoring moment:     M_restore = W * d     (N·m)

2.2 FRICTION AND TRANSPORT
  Friction force:       F_f = mu * N        (N; N = normal force)
  Ramp force:           F = W * (sin(theta) + mu * cos(theta))
  Transport workforce:  n_workers = F_required / F_per_worker
  Sustained human pull: 300 N per person (horizontal, sustained)
  Short-burst human pull: 500 N per person

2.3 FLUID MECHANICS
  Hydrostatic pressure: P = rho * g * h     (Pa; rho_water = 1000 kg/m³)
  Orifice flow:         Q = Cd * A * sqrt(2 * dP / rho)   (m³/s; Cd ~0.6-0.8)
  Manning open channel: Q = (1/n) * A * R^(2/3) * S^(1/2)
                        n = roughness (stone ~0.017), R = hydraulic radius, S = slope
  Self-cleaning velocity: v_min = 0.6 m/s for sediment transport
  Choked flow onset:    P1/P2 >= 1.893 (compressible correction required above this)

2.4 ACOUSTICS
  Speed of sound (air, 20C): c = 343 m/s
  Speed of sound (limestone): c_stone = 4800 m/s
  Echo delay:           t = 2d / c          (d = distance to reflecting surface)
  Flutter echo period:  T = 2L / c          (L = room length)
  Reverberation (Sabine): RT60 = 0.161 * V / (A_s * alpha)
                          V = volume (m³), A_s = surface area, alpha = absorption coefficient
  Resonant frequency (cylinder): f = c / (2L)

2.5 STRUCTURAL
  Corbel stability:     moment of cantilever <= restoring moment of mass above
  Arch thrust:          H = (w * L²) / (8 * f)   (w = load/m, L = span, f = rise)
  Masonry compressive stress: must not exceed 0.1 * f_c (conservative)
  Column bearing:       sigma = P / A
  Terrace wall Ka:      Ka = (1 - sin(phi)) / (1 + sin(phi))   (phi = soil friction angle)
  Active earth pressure: P_a = 0.5 * Ka * gamma * h²

2.6 PRECISION AND DRIFT
  Drift suppression conditions (all must hold simultaneously):
    R >= D              (restoration rate >= drift rate)
    epsilon_obs <= epsilon_required   (observability meets tolerance)
    A_c >= epsilon_bias  (control authority meets bias)
